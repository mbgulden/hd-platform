The 7 Authorities of Human Design

Your Inner Authority is your personal decision-making mechanism — the most reliable compass you have for navigating life correctly. It's not a philosophy or a preference. It's mechanical: determined by which centers are defined in your bodygraph and how they connect. Learning to trust your authority is the single most transformative practice in Human Design.

The seven authorities are: Emotional (Solar Plexus), Sacral, Splenic (Spleen), Ego (Heart), Self-Projected (G Center), Environmental (Mental), and Lunar (Reflector). Each has its own center, rhythm, waiting period, and way of speaking. Discover yours below.

Center: None (Reflector — all centers undefined). A full lunar cycle — approximately 28.

~1% of the population

Why Your Authority Matters More Than Your Strategy

Your Strategy (To Respond, To Inform, Wait for the Invitation, Wait a Lunar Cycle) tells you how to engage with life. Your Authority tells you what is correct for you — it's the internal 'yes' or 'no' that your Strategy relies on. Without following your Authority, your Strategy is blind.

Think of it this way: your Strategy is the vehicle, and your Authority is the navigation system. You can be driving the right vehicle (following your Strategy) but still make wrong turns if you're not reading the navigation system (ignoring your Authority). The two must work together.

How Your Authority Is Determined

Your Inner Authority is not chosen — it's revealed in your chart. The hierarchy is:

  • If your Solar Plexus is defined → Emotional Authority (regardless of other definitions)
  • If your Solar Plexus is undefined but your Sacral is defined → Sacral Authority (Generators and Manifesting Generators)
  • If Solar Plexus and Sacral are undefined but Spleen is defined → Splenic Authority (Manifestors & Projectors)
  • If Solar Plexus, Sacral, and Spleen are undefined but Heart is connected to Throat → Ego Authority (rare, Manifestors)
  • If G Center is connected to Throat with no motor authorities → Self-Projected Authority (Projectors)
  • If no internal authority centers are defined but Ajna/Head are → Environmental (Mental) Authority (Projectors)
  • If no centers are defined at all → Lunar Authority (Reflectors exclusively)

The Authority Spectrum: Internal vs. External

Authorities exist on a spectrum from fastest to slowest. Splenic Authority is the fastest — an instantaneous, one-time whisper. Sacral Authority is also immediate — a gut response in the now. Ego Authority requires a moment of honest self-assessment. Self-Projected Authority requires finding a listener and talking it through. Environmental Authority requires sampling different places. Emotional Authority requires riding the emotional wave — hours to days. Lunar Authority is the slowest — a full 28.5-day lunar cycle.

There is no 'best' authority. Each is perfectly designed for the being who carries it. The only problem is when you try to use someone else's authority — making fast gut decisions when you're Emotional, or waiting for clarity when your Sacral already answered.

Authority in Relationships

One of the most practical applications of understanding authorities is in relationships. When you know your partner's authority is Emotional and yours is Sacral, you understand that they need time while you need in-the-moment response. You stop pressuring them for immediate answers. You stop second-guessing yourself when your gut says yes but they haven't reached clarity yet. Respecting different authorities is one of the most profound acts of love in Human Design.
